Living and Non-Living Things

What are the characteristics of a living thing?

The characteristics of a living thing is that they need water, air, and food (WAF)
they reproduce, they grow, and they die.

What are the characteristics of a non-living thing?

The characteristics of a non-living thing is that they don't need water, air, and food (WAF)
they don't reproduce, they don't grow, and they won't die.

Plants

What are that characteristics of plants?

The characteristics of plants is that they have leaves, a stem, and roots.



Leaves

The main function of leaves is to do photosynthesis (make food).

Stem

The purpose of a stem is to support a plant,
as well as to transport food made by the leaves to
other parts of the plant.

Roots

The functions of roots is to absorb water and nutrients
from the ground as well as anchoring the plant to the ground.

Flowering plants have flowers, which then become fruits.
They reproduce by seeds which are protected by the fruit.

Non-flowering plants have no flowers. They reproduce by spores which are found under the leaves of the plant.
