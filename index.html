Living and Non-Living Things

What are the characteristics of a living thing?

The characteristics of a living thing is that they need water, air, and food (WAF)
they reproduce, they grow, and they die.

What are the characteristics of a non-living thing?

The characteristics of a non-living thing is that they don't need water, air, and food (WAF)
they don't reproduce, they don't grow, and they won't die.

Plants

What are that characteristics of plants?

The characteristics of plants is that they have leaves, a stem, and roots.



Leaves

The main function of leaves is to do photosynthesis (make food).

Stem

The purpose of a stem is to support a plant,
as well as to transport food made by the leaves to
other parts of the plant.

Roots

The functions of roots is to absorb water and nutrients
from the ground as well as anchoring the plant to the ground.

Flowering plants have flowers, which then become fruits.
They reproduce by seeds which are protected by the fruit.

Non-flowering plants have no flowers. They reproduce by spores which are found under the leaves of the plant.



Animals

Animals are living things. There are 6 types of animals: birds, mammals, insects, reptiles, amphibians and fish.


Mammals: The characteristics of mammals is that they have fur/hair(outer covering), and breath through their lungs.
Birds: The characteristics of birds is that they have feathers(outer covering), and breath through their lungs.
Insects: The characteristics of insects is that they have a hard outer covering, and breath through a network of tiny tubes called tracheae.
Reptiles: The characteristics of reptiles is that they have scales(outer covering), and breath through their lungs.
Fish: The characteristics of fish is that they have scales(outer covering), and breath through their gills.
Amphibians: The characteristics of amphibians is that they have smooth skin(outer covering), and breath through their skin.


Fungi and Bacteria

The characteristics of fungi is that they cannot make their own food and they reproduce by spores. They feed on dead or alive things. Not all of them can be seen with the naked eye.
The characteristics of bacteria is that they cannot make their own food and they reproduce by spores. They feed on dead or alive things. Bacteria cannot be seen with the naked eye.
